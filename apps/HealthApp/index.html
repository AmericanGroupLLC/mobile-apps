Definition

What is Health?

Health is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being — not merely the absence of disease or infirmity. It's a holistic balance that empowers people to live full, active, and meaningful lives.

Pillars

Key Types of Health

True wellness is built on three interconnected pillars.

Physical Health

The condition of your body — strength, endurance, flexibility, and the proper functioning of organs and systems.

Mental Health

Cognitive well-being — how you think, learn, focus, and respond to stress, challenges, and the world around you.

Emotional Health

The ability to understand, express, and manage feelings — building resilience and meaningful relationships.

Daily Practice

How Health Is Maintained

Small, consistent habits create lifelong well-being.

01

Balanced Nutrition

Whole foods, fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and proper hydration fuel your body and mind.

02

Regular Exercise

Movement strengthens muscles, supports cardiovascular health, and improves mood and energy.

03

Preventative Care

Regular checkups, screenings, and vaccinations catch issues early and keep you protected.

📱 iPhone vs ⌘ Apple Watch Apps

Most health apps come in two flavors — one on your phone, one on your wrist. Here's how they work together.

1. The “Watch” App on iPhone

A built-in utility that acts as the remote control and settings hub for your Apple Watch.

  • Change watch faces & complications
  • Manage notifications & Do Not Disturb
  • Set up Apple Pay on your wrist
  • Install or remove apps from the watch

⚠️ You cannot delete this app if you use an Apple Watch — it's the bridge between the two devices.

2. watchOS Versions of Apps

When you download a third-party app (Spotify, Strava, Starbucks), you often get two versions: one for iOS and one for watchOS.

  • Designed for quick “glance” interactions
  • Big buttons, minimal text
  • Optimized for 5-second use
  • Often relies on the iPhone app to function fully

🔍 Side-by-Side Comparison

Feature 📱 iOS App (iPhone) ⌘ watchOS App (Apple Watch)
Interface Designed for a large screen — full of detail and menus. Designed for “glances” — quick info and big buttons.
Function The “heavy lifter.” Used for deep configuration and history. The “remote.” Used for quick actions (start a run, pause music).
Independence Works entirely on its own. Most still need the iPhone app installed to work properly.

💡 Why the Same App Feels Different

Even when it's the “same” app (like Apple Maps), the experience is tailored to where it sits.

📱 On the Phone
You type in an address and explore the full map with detail.
⌘ On the Watch
It taps your wrist to tell you “turn left in 100 feet.”
✨ The Short Version

The app on your iPhone is for managing the watch — the apps on your Watch are stripped-down versions of your phone apps, designed to be used in 5 seconds or less.
