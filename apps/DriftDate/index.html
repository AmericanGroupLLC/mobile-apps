Four discovery layers

📍 ZIP

Same ZIP-prefix as you. Tens of people, the people who already share your coffee shop.

🏘 County

Same county. Hundreds — close enough to actually meet, wide enough to pick.

🗺 State

Same state. Thousands — for road trips, transplants, weekend escapes.

🌐 Server

Everyone. Like a prompt instead of a wave; chat unlocks the same way.

The client never sees a precise location — coordinates are fuzzed to ZIP-3 / county / state on-device before any network call.

Three replies, every time you open a chat

CASUAL "Hey — your bookshelf gave it away. What are you reading right now?"
CONTEXT "Saw you're in Santa Clara County too — favorite spot to actually slow down?"
PLAYFUL "Picking the third photo: bold strategy. Tell me more."

A server-side LLM call (provider-agnostic, env-keyed) reads both profiles and the last 5 messages, then returns three suggestions. Tone evolves: slow chat → light prompts; good energy → playful escalation; deep convo → thoughtful follow-up; meetup-ready → public-date suggestions.

Safety, by design

Verified before chat

Live-selfie matched against your reference photo via AWS Rekognition. Only the boolean result is stored — no facial-feature embeddings.

No precise location

Coordinates are truncated to ZIP-3 / county / state on your device. The server has no lat or lon column.

Public-meetup framing

When the conversation reaches meetup-ready, suggestions point at coffee shops and public events — never "send me your address."

Report + block on every screen

One tap from any profile or chat. Blocked users disappear from your discovery, mutual or not.
