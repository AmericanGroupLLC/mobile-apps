How the connectivity ladder works

  1. Local Wi-Fi. Both phones on the same router? BuddyPlay finds you via Bonjour / NSD on _buddyplay._tcp and runs game traffic over a single TCP connection. Lowest latency.
  2. Mobile Hotspot. No router? Host turns on their phone's hotspot, guest joins it. Wi-Fi path resumes — no router required.
  3. Bluetooth LE. No Wi-Fi at all? BLE GATT carries turn-based games. Slower, but it works on a plane, in the subway, or in a basement.

BuddyPlay tries each rung top-down, surfaces a clear status, and lets you pick a rung manually if the auto-detect chooses wrong.

Privacy by absence

No accounts. No servers. No analytics. No ads. No SDKs phoning home. BuddyPlay literally cannot send your data anywhere because BuddyPlay doesn't have anywhere to send it.

Your win/loss tallies live in a local JSON file on your phone. Settings → Erase any time. Done.
